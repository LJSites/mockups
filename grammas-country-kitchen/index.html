Our Story

The fondest memories are gathered around the table — and at Gramma's, that table's been set since the days when the covered wagon out front was the only landmark you needed on the highway. Step through the door and you step back in time: gingham tablecloths, country knick-knacks on every shelf, and coffee that just keeps coming.

We do home cooking the honest way. Eggs and hotcakes and country-fried steak in the morning, burgers and salads come midday, fresh salsa made in the back, and those famous cinnamon rolls so big you'll be planning to share (you won't).

Whether you're a regular or you've just pulled off the freeway on the way to the outlets, you'll get the same welcome: a genuine "good morning," a warm plate, and a whole lot of hospitality.
