The Place

A Proper Diner,
No Apologies

Roy's Cafe sits right on East Main in Barstow — a mile marker for road-trippers who know that a good diner doesn't need to dress itself up. Classic booth seating, a short-order kitchen that moves, and a menu built on the kind of food you actually want after a few hours behind the wheel.

Burgers, mashed potatoes, country gravy, ham and cheese on a flat-top — cooked to order, priced right, served by people who mean it. Route 66 has been changed by chain restaurants and bypass roads, but Roy's is still here doing exactly what it's always done.

What We Make

The Menu

Diner classics, cooked fresh. No tricks, no trends — just good American food made the way it's supposed to be.

  • Pastrami Cheeseburger

    Fresh beef patty loaded with pastrami and grilled onions — the kind of burger that earns its reputation on a road trip.

  • Grilled Ham & Cheese

    Flat-top pressed, golden on the outside. A diner staple executed without shortcuts.

  • Mashed Potatoes & Country Gravy

    Creamy mashed, draped in house-made country gravy. A comfort side that holds its own as a full plate.

  • Onion Rings

    Large cut, crispy batter, served hot. Simple and done right.

  • Carne Asada Street Tacos

    A generous plate of street-style tacos — good for a break from the burger side of the menu.

  • Classic Breakfast Plates

    Eggs, toast, hash browns — the full-service diner breakfast lineup, served from 7 AM.
